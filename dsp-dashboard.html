Analyst notes
Week of 4/1/26 · 4/1–4/5 vs 3/1–3/5 · DeepIntent
DeepIntent has started Q2 slower than expected, driven by supply quality interventions tied to elevated HUMAN IVT rates (~9% by late March). Publisher blocks (5–6 per affected segment) were implemented immediately in coordination with DeepIntent, normalizing IVT rates. Spend patterns are cyclical — quarter resets are typical with mid-month recovery. End-of-quarter over-delivery in March created a tougher Q2 baseline. A HUMAN IVT vendor integration is being explored for structural protection going forward.

Analyst notes
Week of 4/1/26 · 4/1–4/5 vs 3/1–3/5 · Epsilon
Epsilon has started Q2 on the slower side, consistent with typical seasonal softness — ramp usually accelerates mid-quarter. A positive shift: OE spend is picking up (+24% MoM), aligned with our push for more open exchange participation given stronger OpenX economics. Deal spend is down, driven by softness from Wendy's and Walgreens. Epsilon maintains a high bid rate but low CPMs, limiting CTV competitiveness. QBR focus: bid guidance, bid density, Project Beacon (QPS efficiency + addressability), and Dynamic QPS to resolve primetime pipe constraints.
